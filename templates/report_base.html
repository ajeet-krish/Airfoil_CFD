Aerodynamic Principles & Theory

This wind tunnel simulation performs a sweep of the benchmark symmetric {{ naca_name }} airfoil. The sweep spans five key regimes of flight mechanics:

  • 0° Symmetric Baseline: Mirrors top/bottom flow, confirming zero lift.
  • 4° Linear Lift: Clean attached flow matching thin-airfoil lift predictions.
  • 8° High Lift & Pressure Peak: Strong suction peak on the leading edge.
  • 12° Onset of Stall: Developing shear layer instabilities near the trailing edge.
  • 16° Deep Stall / Post-Stall: Severe separation creating a massive recirculating wake.

By visualizing velocity contours with dense streamlines, the transition from fully attached laminarity to turbulent, recirculating wake separation is dramatically captured.

Technical Orchestration Stack

The automation engine executes a fully decoupled modular python pipeline:

  • Geometry Generator: Analytically maps NACA 4-digit profiles to discrete coordinates.
  • Gmsh Mesh Generation: Generates an unstructured finite volume grid utilizing localized boundary layers around the airfoil.
  • SU2 CFD Solver: Solves the compressible Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) equations closed by the Spalart-Allmaras (SA) turbulence model.
  • PyVista Visualizer: Renders off-screen contours and high-density streamlines using the custom Dracula Palette.
